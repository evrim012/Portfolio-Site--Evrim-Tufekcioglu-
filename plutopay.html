Plutopay

PlutoPay is an online banking app that combines traditional finance with block-chain technology. It enables us to provide financial freedom for everyone around the world.

My process

Problem Statement

Current online banking apps do not offer a wallet for Crypto currencies. Fintechs does not support web 3.0 infrastructure for future payment systems.

Solution

Plutopay will offer a wallet system where users will be able to send/receive crypto currencies and also use it as a payment method. We will support web 3.0 developments with a system that will work on Dapp's and DeFi's.

Competitor Analysis

  • Mainly operate in EU zone
  • Over 20 million users use online banking services
  • Limited withdrawal options at ATM’s
  • Subscription model is used widely for additional services
  • Insurance services are offered

    • Our objective is to understand the user and implement their needs to have an app that would sufficiently provide a smooth user experience. We have conducted user surveys followed by user interviews to understand their needs and expectations. Once the app is created we will analyse the analytics to investigate further changes.

    ‘‘Our goal is to create an app that would both be useful and user friendly’’

    Frameworks & Early Wireframes

    After creating wireframes I have made paper and pen sketches to create early wireframes. These wireframes were then made to mid-fidelity on Adobe XD for further user testing

    More User Testing

    My goal is to cross-check results from card research and analyse how clear the app is. I made sure that the site map is on track and adjust it with findings. Scaling the usability of the app and making changes accordingly. All tests were moderated in person and were done online.

    Usability Test Results

    After using Jakob Nielsen’s four-step rating scale it is clear that these major errors should be fixed. It appears that users look for their statements under accounts. Users also had difficulties swapping the card and making card information visible.

    Most important steps we took:

      Add statements under accounts.
      Swiping and clicking on the card name will swap cardsç
      Clicking on a card will show/hide card information.

    Additional steps to take:

      Add the last transaction on the home page and remove the chart.
      Adjustable dashboard menu to allow the user to see what is important.
      Smaller buttons can be tested to see UI related benefits.
      Deposit option could also be added to the accounts section next to the initial account

    To Conclude:

    Overall users think that the app is easy to use and felt familiar with. Users easily locate their cards however some had difficulties finding details or swapping between them. Users expected more information on the dashboard page and some changes will be done accordingly. The most difficult task was to find statements which urgent actions will be taken regarding that.
